Project Introduction: Pipeline to Success Homepage Redesign

Pipeline to Success is a student-driven organization based at the University of Guelph, dedicated to bridging the gap between university life and future career opportunities. Their primary mission is to provide students with career-relevant experiences, skills, and knowledge through a variety of tailored programs and services. The organization operates with a strong commitment to integrity, collaboration, and growth, both for the students they serve and their own team members.

To achieve these objectives, Pipeline to Success offers several core programs:

  • Pipeline to Clinical Experience: Connecting students to valuable clinical volunteering opportunities, easing administrative burdens for clinics, and fostering meaningful partnerships.
  • Pipeline to Research: Providing students with access to lab positions and serving as a platform where professors can find qualified students for their research projects.
  • MCAT@Guelph: Offering structured preparation sessions for students planning to take the MCAT.

For this project, the Pipeline to Success team sought a redesign of their homepage to better reflect their goals and values, provide clear navigation to their various programs, and create a more engaging user experience. The goal was to design a clean, user-friendly interface that captures the essence of their mission and enhances the accessibility of information, news, and updates for their diverse audience, including university students, employers, professors, and clinics.

As part of the redesign, I focused on creating a visually cohesive layout that aligns with their brand identity, incorporating elements that emphasize their core values of service, growth, and integrity. The redesigned homepage now serves as a dynamic hub for students to explore opportunities and for stakeholders to connect with talented individuals.

Insights from User Interviews

During the initial phase of the redesign, I conducted user interviews to gather insights on what the new design should incorporate. Key takeaways from the interviews included:

  • Clear Objectives: The need to clearly present their diverse programs and upcoming events.
  • User-Friendly Navigation: A clean and intuitive layout for easy access to important information.
  • Focus on Core Values: Emphasis on the organizationâ€™s mission of service, growth, and integrity.
  • Future Programs: The importance of highlighting upcoming initiatives like Pipeline to Industry and Academic Contests.

Problem Statement & Design Goals

The old design of the "Pipeline to Success" website presented several challenges that affected user engagement and navigation. The primary issues were:

  • Cluttered Navigation: Users found it hard to locate important information due to a disorganized layout.
  • Lack of Visual Hierarchy: Text-heavy pages made it difficult for users to quickly grasp key content.
  • Poor User Engagement: The old design lacked interactive elements that could keep users engaged and encourage them to take action.

Design Goals: To address these issues, the redesign focused on the following goals:

  • Simplified Navigation: Creating a more organized and accessible menu structure to improve user experience.
  • Enhanced Visual Hierarchy: Utilizing clear headings, subheadings, and visual elements to make key information stand out.
  • Improved User Engagement: Introducing interactive elements, such as call-to-action buttons, to encourage users to participate in programs and join the community.

Old Design Analysis: Pipeline to Success Website

The old design of the "Pipeline to Success" website had several usability and visual design issues that affected user engagement and navigation. Below are screenshots of the old design along with a detailed analysis of the problems identified in each section:

1. About Page

Problem: The About page lacks visual hierarchy and engaging elements. The text-heavy format makes it difficult for users to quickly grasp key information about the organization.

2. Mailing List Section

Problem: The call to action for joining the mailing list is not prominent enough. The design lacks visual contrast, making it easy for users to overlook this important element.

3. Clinical Experience Section

Problem: The section is text-heavy and lacks visual cues to guide users. This results in low engagement and difficulty in understanding the value of the program at a glance.

4. Upcoming Initiatives

Problem: Upcoming initiatives are buried under large chunks of text, making it hard for users to quickly find relevant information.

5. Testimonials

Problem: The testimonials section lacks structure and hierarchy, which makes the quotes feel less impactful and the page cluttered.

6. Join the Team Section

Problem: The design of this section does not adequately convey the call to action. The text is lengthy and lacks visual emphasis on the benefits of joining the team.

7. Workshops

Problem: The Workshops page is extremely minimal with little information provided. It fails to engage users or inform them about the available workshops effectively.

8. Research Section

Problem: Similar to other sections, the Research section is text-heavy and lacks interactive elements that would enhance user engagement and comprehension.

9. Employment Section

Problem: The Employment section does not stand out due to its plain design and lack of interactive elements, making it less effective in attracting students to explore job opportunities.

Brand Identity

The new brand identity for the "Pipeline to Success" website includes a well-defined monochromatic color scheme, consistent components, and visual hierarchy.

Wireframe

Low-fidelity wireframes were created to illustrate the new layout and user flow. Key design decisions, such as navigation placement, CTAs, and content sections, were strategically planned to enhance the user experience.

High-Fidelity Mockup

Polished, high-fidelity mockups were created for the redesigned homepage and other key pages, focusing on visual hierarchy, color scheme, typography, and interactive elements.

Outcome & Impact

The redesign resulted in improved user engagement metrics, such as increased time on site, reduced bounce rates, and positive feedback from users and stakeholders.
